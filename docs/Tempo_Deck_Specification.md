# Tempo — Slide Deck Specification
## CICDC 2026 Digital Marketing Strategies

**Total slides:** 16 (+ 1 transition slide = 17)
**Aspect ratio:** 16:9
**Deck length target:** 11:00 minutes presenting
**Design system:** See Brand Direction section at end

---

## SLIDE 1 — Title/Hook Holding Slide

**Time on screen:** 0:00-0:30

**Purpose:** First visual impression. Judge forms 45-point perception in these seconds.

**Layout:**
- Full-bleed background: warm neutral (soft sand, #E8DFD2)
- Centered: Tempo wordmark (custom-lettered or Söhne Breit), dark charcoal (#1A1A1A)
- Below wordmark, smaller: "Clothes that move at your pace."
- Bottom-left corner: "Desta & Yishak Consulting — CICDC 2026" in small type
- No photography on title slide — restraint signals confidence

**Design notes:**
- Typography does the work. No decoration.
- Wordmark should have slight custom character (extended T crossbar, or altered O that evokes a tempo/metronome mark)
- Absolutely no stock imagery

---

## SLIDE 2 — "The 15-Minute Morning"

**Time on screen:** 1:00-1:30

**Purpose:** Anchor the emotional hook in a single statement.

**Layout:**
- Text-dominant slide, 75% whitespace
- Headline centered, large: "The 15-Minute Morning"
- No subtitle, no image, no body copy
- Warm neutral background, same palette as Slide 1

**Design notes:**
- This slide is a moment. The visual silence amplifies the spoken story.
- Typography: serif display font (Playfair Display or similar) — this slide reads like the title of a short story
- Do not add anything else. Restraint is the message.

---

## SLIDE 3 — "90 Hours Per Year"

**Time on screen:** 1:30-2:00

**Purpose:** Convert emotion to arithmetic. Judges respond to quantification.

**Layout:**
- Large number "90" in deep charcoal, occupying 50% of slide area
- Below, smaller: "hours per year per person"
- Supporting text in bottom third: "15 minutes × 2 times/day × 365 days = 90 hours"
- Right side: small secondary stat card: "$14/hour — home care aide wage"

**Design notes:**
- Number should feel monumental. Think editorial magazine, not infographic.
- Single accent color on the number (muted amber, #C29E5F)
- Math breakdown small enough that judge has to lean in

---

## SLIDE 4 — The Customer Scale

**Time on screen:** 2:00-2:30

**Purpose:** Widen the lens from one morning to a market. Transitions Tesfa → Bityana.

**Layout:**
- Three stat blocks stacked vertically, each building on the previous:
  - "1.3 billion" / "people living with disability globally" / source tag: "WHO, 2023"
  - "61 million" / "disabled Americans" / source tag: "CDC, 2024"
  - "53 million" / "US family caregivers" / source tag: "AARP, 2024"
- Right side: thin geographic motif (abstract world map shape in 5% opacity, not literal)

**Design notes:**
- Each stat should be a distinct visual block — they appear sequentially if the deck supports animation
- Source attribution small but visible. Unsourced numbers are a Q&A liability.
- Restraint on the map — it should feel like a ghost, not a dashboard

---

## SLIDE 5 — Market Sizing Chart

**Time on screen:** 2:30-3:00

**Purpose:** Hard market data. 15-point Value/Benefit/Price rubric category.

**Layout:**
- Upper 2/3: bar chart showing global adaptive clothing market, $17.7B in 2024 growing to $28B by 2030
- CAGR label: "6-7% annually" overlaid on slope
- Lower 1/3: four-cell unit-economics table — CAC, Conversion, AOV, LTV — comparing Mainstream DTC Fashion vs. Adaptive category
- Source footer: "Fortune Business Insights, Grand View Research, Upcounting 2025"

**Design notes:**
- Use your brand's accent color for the Adaptive bars/values
- Mainstream numbers in muted grey
- Chart should be typeset cleanly — no default Excel aesthetics
- The visual story: adaptive is bigger AND has better economics

---

## SLIDE 6 — Competitive Whitespace Matrix

**Time on screen:** 3:00-4:00

**Purpose:** THE money slide. This is what judges will remember.

**Layout:**
- Grid: rows = competitor brands (Tommy Adaptive, Nike FlyEase, IZ Adaptive, June Adaptive, Joe & Bella, Silverts, Unhidden, Tempo), columns = four pillars (Adaptive-First, Sustainable Materials, DPP Technology, Caregiver Experience)
- Cells: filled circle (has pillar), empty circle (does not), partial circle (partial)
- Tempo row at bottom, visually distinct — accent color fill, slightly larger row
- Tempo row has all four filled circles. No other brand has more than two.

**Design notes:**
- This slide needs to be scannable in 3 seconds. Judge should see the gap instantly.
- Grid lines thin and grey. Circles large and clean.
- Optional callout: "Every competitor has picked two pillars. Tempo picks four."
- Do NOT use checkmarks/Xs — filled/empty circles are more elegant

**Design notes:**
- Same treatment as Slide 1 but without the Desta & Yishak attribution at bottom — this is about Tempo now

## SLIDE 8 — Four-Pillar Positioning

**Time on screen:** 5:00-5:30

**Purpose:** Claim the whitespace explicitly.

**Layout:**
- Four vertical columns of equal width, each with a pillar header + 2-3 supporting phrases
- Column 1 — **Adaptive Design**: "Visible, not hidden · Designed with disabled advisors · No sartorial assimilation"
- Column 2 — **Sustainable Materials**: "GOTS organic cotton · Fair Trade certified · Recycled polyester blends"
- Column 3 — **Digital Product Passport**: "GS1 Digital Link standard · JSON-LD open data · QR-scannable on every garment"
- Column 4 — **Caregiver-First UX**: "Caregiver Mode toggle · Time-to-dress labeling · Condition-specific filtering"

**Design notes:**
- Each column gets a subtle accent color variation — four related tones, not four bright primaries
- Dividing lines between columns should be hairline thin
- Icons above each header optional but if used, they should be custom SVG (not stock icons)

## SLIDE 9 — Website Homepage

**Time on screen:** 5:30-6:00

**Purpose:** Visual proof that the strategy ships.

**Layout:**
- Full-bleed homepage screenshot, slightly angled/perspective for visual interest (5-10° tilt)
- Subtle overlay with callouts: "WCAG 2.1 AA" / "Adaptive-first navigation" / "Keyboard-only flows"
- Bottom-left corner: URL — "tempo.style" or your actual deployed domain

**Design notes:**
- If the live site doesn't look good on the big screen, take screenshots of hero + one or two feature sections and composite them as a single vertical scroll
- Callouts should be small and elegant, not sales-y
- This is the first slide that shows product. The leap from strategy to execution lives here.

## SLIDE 10 — AI Fit Concierge

**Time on screen:** 6:00-6:30

**Purpose:** Show the AI-powered experience that differentiates.

**Layout:**
- Mobile device mockup (angled, 15°) showing a chat conversation
- Above the phone: "AI Fit Concierge — powered by Claude Sonnet"
- Conversation visible on screen (example):
  - User: "My mom is in a wheelchair after a stroke, has limited use of her right hand. She wants to look put-together for family dinner."
  - Tempo: "For easy one-handed dressing with dignity, I'd recommend our Seated-Cut Trouser in charcoal ($89, magnetic side-closure) paired with the Knotless Wrap Blouse ($72). Both are sensory-friendly and the trouser has seat-relief tailoring for wheelchair comfort. Want me to check availability in her size?"

**Design notes:**
- The conversation matters more than the mockup. Spend time on realistic, specific, non-generic AI dialogue.
- Uses the vocabulary disabled caregivers actually use (not "person with disabilities")
- Phone mockup should be iPhone (most judges will be iPhone users)

## SLIDE 11 — DPP Passport Page

**Time on screen:** 6:30-7:00

**Purpose:** The technical credibility play. If one slide earns the "EU DPP 2027" talking point, it's this one.

**Layout:**
- Left half: product photo of a hero garment, with a physical QR code visible on the tag
- Right half: the live DPP passport page — structured info including fiber composition, factory location, carbon footprint, care/sterilization instructions, certifications (GOTS, Fair Trade)
- Thin horizontal callout at bottom: "ESPR-compliant · GS1 Digital Link · Ready for EU 2027 mandate"

**Design notes:**
- The QR code should be functional. Judge may scan it live.
- The data should look like *data* — tabular, clean, technical. Not a marketing page.
- If possible, embed an actual GTIN number that a judge could look up

## SLIDE 12 — Caregiver Mode

**Time on screen:** 7:00-7:30

**Purpose:** Feature no competitor has. Defensibility moment.

**Layout:**
- Split screen: left shows standard product listing ("Shop All Men's"), right shows the same listing with Caregiver Mode activated — new filters visible ("Condition: Post-Stroke · Time-to-Dress: Under 5 min · Sterilization-safe")
- Center divider with a toggle graphic: "Caregiver Mode: OFF / ON"
- Top caption: "One toggle. Entire experience changes."

**Design notes:**
- This slide should feel like before/after. The transformation is the message.
- Highlight 3-4 new UI elements on the right side with small accent markers
- Do not overexplain — judge should see the difference instantly

**Design notes:**

**Time on screen:** 8:15-9:00

**Purpose:** Depth of digital strategy. Email is underrated; partnerships are trust accelerants.

**Layout:**
- Upper half: three parallel lifecycle tracks displayed as horizontal flows

  - Track 2 — Caregiver: Welcome → Caregiver Resource Hub → Care-Facility Intro → B2B Account

**Design notes:**
- Flows should be simple arrows, not complex funnel diagrams
- Open rates and engagement stats in small type (not the focus)
- Partnership logos in greyscale to signal restraint — we're not selling the partners, we're showing due diligence

## Brand Direction

### Typography
- **Headlines:** Playfair Display (serif, editorial feel)
- **Body:** Inter or Söhne (modern sans, high legibility)
- **Wordmark:** Custom-lettered version of Söhne Breit, or commissioned
- **Maximum 2 typefaces per slide**

### Color Palette
- **Primary background:** Warm sand (#E8DFD2)
- **Secondary background:** Off-white (#FAFAF7)
- **Primary text:** Deep charcoal (#1A1A1A)
- **Accent 1:** Muted amber (#C29E5F) — for numbers, emphasis
- **Accent 2:** Sage (#7A8B75) — for positive states, growth bars
- **Accent 3:** Terracotta (#C4725A) — sparingly, for alerts/highlights
- **Never:** bright primaries, pure black, pure white

### Motion/Animation
- Minimize transitions. Fade or simple reveal only.
- No spinning, flying, or complex animations.
- If the deck is in Keynote/PowerPoint, use Magic Move only for intentional callbacks (e.g., Slide 16 calling back to Slide 1)

### Photography
- **Required if used:** disabled models, seated bodies, diverse skin tones, real caregivers
- **Never:** stock photography of smiling disabled people (ablesaviorism trope)
- **Prefer:** editorial, natural light, confident posture, direct-to-camera gaze
- **If unable to source properly:** use typography and illustration instead — visual restraint beats bad photography

### Iconography
- Custom SVG icons only where used
- No emoji, no icon fonts, no Noun Project generics

### Accessibility of the Deck Itself
- Minimum 18pt body, 32pt headlines
- Color contrast ratio 4.5:1 minimum on all text
- Alt text added to every image in case judges request accessible version

## Physical Materials (produced alongside deck)

### Business Cards
- Both partners' names on one side, consulting firm on other
- QR code on back linking to tempo.style
- Recycled cotton stock (lives the brand)
- Sober, professional — no gimmicks

### One-Pager Leave-Behind
- 8.5x11", printed front-and-back on sustainable paper
- Front: Brand overview in the tone of the pitch (home care opening, 4-pillar framework, key stats)
- Back: Financial model summary + contact info
- Should look like a business document, not a brochure

### Printed Mockups (optional but impactful)
- 3-5 small printed cards showing key mockups from the deck (website, DPP passport, AI Concierge)
- Acts as tactile proof points
- Fit in a small folder you leave on the judge's table
